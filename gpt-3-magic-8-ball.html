GPT-3 Magic 8 Ball
Watch on Vimeo
The video shows a web interface with a floating eight ball and the prompt, "Ask the mystical magic eight ball." The input field asks, "What would you like to know?"

This is a simple web application I created to play with OpenAI's GPT-3 API. I am presenting it in video form because OpenAI currently has very strict guidelines about exposing the API publicly and I don't have the motivation to implement the necessary features it would take to go live.

I am using the davinci completion endpoint, and this is my prompt data object if you are able to try it in the playground yourself:

{ prompt: "I am the mystical magic eight ball. Ask me any question and I will tell you the answer.\n<q>What will the weather be like tomorrow?<a>Overcast skies <enda>\n<q>What's my favorite color?<a>Blue.<enda>\n<q>Am I going to win the lottery?<a>If you play the right numbers<enda>\n<q>" + question, temperature: 1, max_tokens: 50, top_p: 1, frequency_penalty: 0, presence_penalty: 0, stop: ["\n"] }

When I gained access to the GPT-3 private beta, one of the most surprising things I found in the beta-testing community was its faith in the model's pre-training. I wanted to explore the hidden knowledge of AI systems, tricking them into telling us that they know more than they let on. With the vast stores of knowledge that GPT-3 has been trained on, could it tell the future?

I asked GPT-3 to play the role of a Magic 8 Ball in the prompt, but did not supply it with any of the 20 canonical Magic 8 Ball answers so that it would answer to the best of its ability within the role.
